How to use this trainer

This drill builds one skill: figuring out how many minutes before your destination you need to begin a descent so you arrive at the traffic pattern altitude (TPA) — descending at a steady 500 feet per minute.

  1. Read the Altimeter card. This is your current cruise altitude in feet above sea level (MSL). It's shown both on the round gauge and as a digital number.
  2. Read the Traffic Pattern Altitude card. This is the altitude (MSL) you need to level off at when you reach the destination's traffic pattern.
  3. Work the math yourself before peeking. Find how much altitude you need to lose, then how long that takes at 500 ft/min. Write your answer down.
  4. Click "Show answer" to check your work and see the steps laid out.
  5. Click "New scenario" for a fresh set of numbers and repeat. Aim to do the math in your head as you get faster.

The method, every time:

altitude to lose = cruise altitude − TPA
descent time (min) = altitude to lose ÷ 500

That descent time is your answer: it's how many minutes out you start down. On a GPS like the GNS 430, it's also the ETE (estimated time en route) value you watch for — when the ETE counts down to that number, it's time to begin your descent.

Tip: 500 ft/min means you lose 1,000 ft every 2 minutes. So a 4,500 ft descent takes 9 minutes. Try estimating before you reach for a calculator.
